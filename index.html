About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Dalian University of Technology, working on computer vision, video understanding, and representation learning. My research interests include multimodal large language models, video understanding, image recognition, few-shot learning, and visual representation learning.

My work has been published in leading conferences and journals, including CVPR, NeurIPS, IJCV, ACM MM, and IEEE TNNLS. My publications have received more than 1,100 citations.

Research Projects

Zero-Shot Action Recognition with LLM-Based Prompts

Mar. 2025 – Jun. 2025

  • Designed semantic prompts with intra-class attributes and inter-class differences using LLaMA.
  • Injected language-driven semantics into visual and textual encoders for cross-modal alignment.
  • Improved discriminability and generalization in zero-shot video recognition.

Adaptively Aligned Multi-Scale Moment for Few-Shot Video Recognition

May 2022 – Jun. 2024 · IJCV 2025

  • Addressed temporal misalignment in few-shot video matching.
  • Built a multi-scale spatio-temporal moment module for hierarchical dynamic modeling.
  • Achieved state-of-the-art performance on few-shot video recognition benchmarks.

Temporal-Attentive Covariance Pooling for Video Recognition

May 2020 – May 2021 · NeurIPS 2021

  • Introduced temporal attention to highlight temporally sensitive regions.
  • Modeled intra-frame and inter-frame relations with covariance representations.
  • Designed a flexible module compatible with multiple 2D and 3D CNN architectures.

Global Second-Order Pooling for Image Recognition

May 2018 – Nov. 2018 · CVPR 2019

  • Developed a plug-and-play global second-order pooling module.
  • Explicitly modeled channel correlations through covariance representations.
  • Promoted second-order statistics for visual representation learning.
